KCS v6 / Frontline-owned content

Knowledge-Centered Service

The industry standard for shifting content creation from centralized teams to the people doing the work. A central team governs and audits the system. Adopted by ServiceNow, Cisco, Oracle, Dell, and Salesforce. Directly tied to customer experience improvements through faster resolution and higher first-contact resolution.

Developed by Gottfredson and Mosher. Defines when formal training is needed versus just-in-time support. When cognitive load is high and error tolerance is low, the format must match the gap type. This is how CS domain expertise translates into the right learning modality at the right speed.

What frontline owns

Problem solving and content execution

Frontline identifies gaps, scopes issues, drafts corrections with the Content Readiness Agent, reviews outputs, publishes to the knowledge base, and owns change communications. This includes: using QA data and Project FixIt to identify gaps, drafting within the FCS Writing Style Guide, managing peer review through the agent, publishing via Contentful/Bloomfire, and pushing updates via CCO NewsFeed.

What Content owns

Automated governance and systemic oversight

Content builds and maintains an automated governance layer that validates published content against defined standards on a cadence (monthly or biweekly). This runs separately from the creation-to-publish pipeline. Content does not create, prioritize, or rewrite. They design the automated control system, perform cadence-based reviews to catch drift, and provide systemic insight across the full content base.

Recommendation 2 / L&D

Route every gap to the right modality at the right speed.

We are shifting to a model where CS domain expertise identifies the gap type, AI-driven actions match it to the right learning modality, and delivery happens at the speed each demands.

Three lanes, matched to gap type

Reinforce

Knowledge gaps

Advocates don't know, don't remember, or can't find the answer fast enough. Quick refreshers, job aids, microlearning, or custom materials built by frontline from the co-built library or generated using AI tools like Goose. Frontline builds. L&D publishes to Block Academy, advocates enrolled.

Practice

Skill gaps

Advocates understand the concept but can't execute consistently under real-time pressure. Simulation scenarios, role-plays, and applied exercises built by L&D using tools across the learning tech stack, including in-house builds and platforms like Reflex.ai. Frontline defines requirements. This is a performance issue that partners own closing.

Build

Complex or new

New products, policy changes, sensitive scenarios, or entirely new capabilities that don't have a foundation to build on. Structured learning and facilitated sessions. L&D designs, builds, and delivers. This is where their instructional design expertise is irreplaceable.

Functional expertise in this model

How Content and L&D work differently.

Both teams bring expertise the system depends on. This model redeploys that expertise toward system design, governance, and acceleration, where it creates the most value for customer experience, rather than day-to-day production.

Content's superpower

Governance system design

Content builds and maintains the automated governance layer. They perform reviews that identify systemic patterns across the full content base. They improve standards and the system over time. Their editorial expertise makes the governance layer smarter and more precise. Instead of spending capacity on individual articles and rewrites, Content's expertise scales through the system they build. One well-designed governance layer covers every publish. Their time goes to systemic insight, not production.

L&D's superpower

Learning architecture

L&D co-builds the artifact library with Frontline CX, setting learning standards and frameworks that define quality. They design practice exercises and simulations from frontline specs. They own complex facilitation. Their instructional design expertise powers the modality framework and the library. Instead of processing a queue of mixed-complexity requests, L&D's capacity goes to high-impact work: co-building the library that powers reinforcement deployments, designing practice scenarios that address real skill gaps, and facilitating training only they can deliver.
